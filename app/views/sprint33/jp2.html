Admin assistant

Alternative titles for this job include Office administrator, clerical assistant, administrative assistant

Admin assistants give support to offices by organising meetings, typing documents and updating computer records.

Average salary (a year)

£14,000 Starter

to

£30,000 Experienced

Typical hours (a week)

38 to 40 a week

Typical working pattern

between 8am and 6pm

Employment outlook

Future demand for this job group (until 2024)

+ 5.3% Nationally

How to become an admin assistant

You can get into this job through:

  • a college course
  • an apprenticeship
  • working towards this role
  • applying directly

College

You could do a college course, which would teach you some of the skills and knowledge you need in this job. Relevant subjects include a Level 2 or 3 Diploma in Business and Administration.

Entry requirements

You'll usually need:

  • 2 or more GCSEs at grades 9 to 3 (A* to D) for a level 2 course
  • 4 or 5 GCSEs at grades 9 to 4 (A* to C) for a level 3 course

Apprenticeship

You could get into this job through an intermediate apprenticeship in business administration, or a business administrator advanced apprenticeship.

Entry requirements

You'll usually need:

  • some GCSEs, usually including English and maths, for an intermediate apprenticeship
  • 5 GCSEs at grades 9 to 4 (A* to C), or equivalent, including English and maths, for an advanced apprenticeship

Work

You might be able to start on a temporary contract through a recruitment agency. Sometimes this can lead to a permanent job. Qualifications in business administration may also help.

Volunteering

You could volunteer with a charity in an office based role to get some experience and skills that are useful for administration work.

Read our guidance about how to obtain work experience, internships and volunteer work to get started.

Direct application

You could apply directly to become an admin assistant.

Some employers may expect you to have:

  • GCSEs at grades 9 to 4 (A* to C) or equivalent, including English and maths
  • telephone, typing or IT skills

What it takes

Skills and knowledge

You'll need:

  • administration skills
  • to be thorough and pay attention to detail
  • the ability to work well with others
  • the ability to work on your own
  • sensitivity and understanding
  • to be flexible and open to change
  • excellent verbal communication skills
  • to be able to use a computer and the main software packages competently

A day in the life of an admin assistant

The National Careers Service spoke to Beth, an administrative assistant in a secondary school in Ely.

What does a day in your life look like?

8.00am: Already people are waiting in the office that have critical needs. I speak to several parents with enquiries and several students with timetable queries. The phone rings every 5 minutes with simple requests that take my time until 10am.

My boss calls and lets me know he has a meeting in another town — could I get a report off his desk, scan it and send it to his phone?

10.00am: Finally I can check my email and review the stack of work left from yesterday.

10.15am: I start on report for the school board, respond to a request for state reports on attendance and teacher time in classroom, and organize a new time schedule that meets state requirements and community requests.

11.00am: Post information to the school website.

11.30pm: My boss returns from his meeting—he needs me to contact several people for a meeting tomorrow. I call them and leave messages for the people who aren’t available.

12.00pm: I eat lunch at my desk, while answering the phone and working on my to-do list.

2.30pm: My co-worker asks me to help organize a retirement party for a supervisor — she really means will I arrange a time and place, make a list of invitees, prepare and mail invitations and get prices from the caterer. She will handle the program.

4.00pm: Review my to-do list and see several projects that had to be completed today. I work until 5:30 to complete them and vow to be better organized tomorrow.

What you'll do

Day-to-day tasks

In this role you could be:

  • dealing with queries on the phone, by email and social media
  • greeting visitors at reception
  • typing letters and reports
  • updating computer records
  • printing and photocopying
  • ordering supplies
  • setting up meetings and taking notes during them
  • making travel arrangements for staff

Working environment

You could work in an office.

Career path and progression

With experience, you could progress from admin assistant to supervisor or office manager. You could also move into other departments, like IT, payroll or accounting.

With further training, you could specialise in an area like:

  • legal administration
  • financial administration
  • medical administration

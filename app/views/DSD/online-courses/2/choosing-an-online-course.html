Choosing an online course

Learning online or e-learning is a way to improve your skills without leaving your home.

Online learning explained

Online learning takes place over the internet instead of in a classroom. It’s a flexible way to fit learning around your daily life. You can study subjects that are useful for most jobs like:

  • maths
  • English
  • how to use a computer

There are also courses for developing skills that are specific to particular jobs. There could be a course that suits your interests and current level of skill. Use our find a course tool to explore free online courses that may be available now.

You’ll usually enrol online and get access to a website where you can find course materials. Some courses have an online tutor. You may also have the chance to get to know other learners through a ‘virtual classroom’ or group chat.

What to look for in an online course

You should make sure:

  • the course provider has a good reputation - ask for recommendations
  • you understand the course details and entry requirements
  • what support you’ll get from an online tutor
  • If you’ll get a certificate or qualification at the end

When signing up for an online course you should check:

  • when you can start
  • Whether there will be any cost
  • the methods you will use to learn - is it the right method for you (for example, group work)?
  • how long the course will be
  • what course materials the provider will give you or what you need to buy
